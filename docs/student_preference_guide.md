# Student Preference Configuration Guide

This guide helps students use the recommendation demo effectively.

## Inputs that matter most

- **Entrance exam**: choose the exam pathway you are actually applying through.
- **Rank**: use your realistic best rank, not your target rank.
- **Preferred branches**: include 1-3 branches you would genuinely accept.
- **Budget**: enter the maximum yearly education cost you can realistically sustain.
- **Preferred states/cities**: use these to express relocation comfort, not hard dreams only.
- **Hostel preference**: enable this if campus accommodation meaningfully affects your decision.

## Suggested usage pattern

1. Start broad.
2. Review the top-ranked colleges and reasons.
3. Read the official evidence snippets and citations.
4. Narrow by branch, location, and budget after you understand your realistic option set.

## How to interpret the output

- A **high match score** means the college fits your academic and practical constraints well.
- **Why it matches you** explains how rank, branch, location, budget, and hostel preference affected the ranking.
- **Official evidence snapshot** gives short grounded context from the RAG system.
- **Official citations** are the sources you should open before acting on the recommendation.

## Good decision hygiene

- Treat the shortlist as a planning tool, not a guaranteed admission prediction.
- Compare multiple colleges instead of stopping at the top result.
- Prefer official sources over hearsay for fees, counselling, hostel rules, and eligibility.
